1. Overview – Key Points

  • No account required: We do not require you to create an account or sign in.
  • Data stored mainly on your device: Caller profiles, schedules, and app settings are stored locally (such as SharedPreferences).
  • No real call routing: The App uses Android's Telecom APIs and local simulation only. It does not route real calls through servers.
  • No call log access: Shadow Call does not read or write your device call history.
  • Limited use of personal data: Personal information is used only to provide fake call and dialer simulation features.
  • Third-party services: Google AdMob (ads) and Google Play Billing (in-app purchases) may collect data under their own policies.

2. Information We Collect and How We Use It

2.1 Information You Provide

  • Caller profiles and presets — fake caller name, number, optional photo/label. Stored locally.
  • Scheduled call details — caller profile, time, repeat mode, alarm IDs. Stored locally and in Android alarm storage.
  • App settings — vibration, auto-answer/hangup, ringtone, SIM labels. Stored locally.
  • Custom audio / text-to-speech — recordings and TTS text for fake calls. Stored locally; not uploaded.

We process this information only to provide the App's core features and do not sell it.

2.2 Information from Device Permissions

Shadow Call requests permissions incrementally, in context, when a feature needs them. You can revoke permissions anytime in Android settings.

Phone & call simulation Examples: READ_PHONE_STATE, MANAGE_OWN_CALLS, Telecom / calling account capabilities.
  • Register and manage a phone account for simulated fake calls through Android Telecom.
  • Display incoming call UI using the system's call screen.
  • Manage simulated call lifecycle (ringing, answered, ended).
Shadow Call does not place real cellular calls and does not use CALL_PHONE, READ_CALL_LOG, or WRITE_CALL_LOG.
Contacts (system picker only)
  • Shadow Call does not request broad READ_CONTACTS access.
  • When you tap "Pick from contacts," the Android system contact picker opens and you choose one contact.
  • Only the contact you select is used to fill the caller name and number.
You can always type caller details manually without using the contact picker.
Microphone (optional) Example: RECORD_AUDIO.
  • Requested only when you record custom voice for conversation simulation.
  • Recordings are stored locally and are not uploaded to our servers.
Exact alarm and scheduling Examples: SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM, RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED.
  • Schedule fake calls at exact times.
  • Restore scheduled fake calls after device reboot where supported.
Foreground service and notifications Examples: FOREGROUND_SERVICE, FOREGROUND_SERVICE_PHONE_CALL, POST_NOTIFICATIONS, USE_FULL_SCREEN_INTENT.
  • Run foreground services to reliably trigger fake calls in the background.
  • Show notifications and full-screen incoming call UI when required by Android.

2.3 Automatically Collected Information

  • On-device technical data: Local logs for schedules, errors, and call simulation state (debugging/reliability).
  • Google AdMob: Device identifiers, approximate location (IP-based), ad interactions — per Google's policies.

We do not maintain our own backend server for tracking your usage across devices.

3. In-App Purchases

Shadow Call may offer in-app purchases (e.g. remove ads) via Google Play Billing. Google processes purchase tokens and payment details; we receive only purchase success/failure to unlock features. See Google's Privacy Policy.

4. Third-Party Services and Data Sharing

  • Google Mobile Ads (AdMob) — advertisements.
  • Google Play Services / Billing — distribution and purchases.

We do not sell your personal data.

5. Data Storage, Retention, and Security

  • Local storage: Caller profiles, schedules, settings, and logs stay on your device. Uninstalling the App generally removes this data.
  • Retention: Only as long as needed for app functionality or legal obligations.
  • Security: Reasonable measures; absolute security cannot be guaranteed.

6. Your Choices and Controls

  • Permissions: Grant or revoke Phone, Microphone, or Alarms in Android settings. Without phone permission, system call UI may not work; without microphone, you cannot record custom voice.
  • Manual entry: You can enter caller names and numbers without using the contact picker.
  • Local data: Delete presets/schedules in the app or uninstall to remove local data.
  • Ads: Control ad personalization via device/Google settings where available.

7. Children's Privacy
