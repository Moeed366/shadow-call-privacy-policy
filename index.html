1. Overview – Key Points

  • No account required: We do not require you to create an account or sign in.
  • Data stored mainly on your device: Caller profiles, schedules, and app settings are stored locally on your device using local storage (such as SharedPreferences).
  • No real call routing: The App uses Android's Telecom APIs and local simulation only. It does not route real calls through servers.
  • Limited use of personal data: Personal information (like contact names or numbers) is used only to provide fake call and dialer simulation features.
  • Third-party services: We use services such as Google AdMob (ads) and Google Play Billing (in-app purchases). These services may collect and process data under their own privacy policies.

2. Information We Collect and How We Use It

2.1 Information You Provide

  • Caller profiles and presets
    Data: Fake caller name, phone number, optional photo, optional label.
    Use: To generate believable fake incoming calls and caller presets.
    Storage: Saved locally on your device.
  • Scheduled call details
    Data: Caller profile, scheduled time, repeat mode, and internal alarm identifiers.
    Use: To schedule and trigger fake incoming calls at the times you choose.
    Storage: Saved locally on your device and in Android alarm storage to work across app restarts and reboots.
  • App settings and preferences
    Data: Vibration preference, auto-answer timer, auto-hangup timer, ringtone selection, SIM-slot display label, and similar configuration options.
    Use: To control how fake calls behave and how the App is displayed.
    Storage: Saved locally on your device.
  • Custom audio and text-to-speech (if you use those features)
    Data: Audio you record (e.g., for custom ringtones or call audio) and text you enter for text-to-speech playback.
    Use: To play your chosen audio or synthesized voice during fake calls.
    Storage: Stored locally on your device. We do not upload this audio to our own servers.

We process this information only to provide the App's core features and do not sell it.

2.2 Information from Device Permissions

To provide realistic fake call behavior, Shadow Call requests several Android permissions. Depending on your Android version, some are granted at install time and others via runtime prompts. You can change permissions at any time in your device settings, but some features may stop working or become less reliable.

We use permissions only for the purposes described below:

Phone & call-related permissions Examples: READ_PHONE_STATE, MANAGE_OWN_CALLS, Telecom / calling account related capabilities.
  • To register and manage a phone account for fake calls through Android Telecom.
  • To display incoming call UI using the system's call screen.
  • To manage the lifecycle of simulated calls (ringing, answered, ended).
Note: Shadow Call does not place real cellular calls and does not use CALL_PHONE.
Call log permissions Examples: READ_CALL_LOG, WRITE_CALL_LOG.
  • To optionally insert entries into the system call log for fake incoming and outgoing calls, when supported and permitted by your device.
  • To provide a more realistic call history experience.
Note: If you deny these permissions, fake calls may not appear in your system call log.
Contacts permission Example: READ_CONTACTS (read-only).
  • To suggest contacts and numbers from your address book inside the dialer simulation.
  • To allow you to quickly select existing contacts for fake caller profiles or outgoing simulations.
Contact data is read as needed and is not uploaded to our servers. Shadow Call does not modify or write to your contacts.
Microphone permission Example: RECORD_AUDIO.
  • To record optional custom voice audio for conversation simulation during fake calls.
Recordings are stored locally on your device and are not uploaded to our servers.
Exact alarm and scheduling permissions Examples: SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM, RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED.
  • To schedule fake calls at exact times, including while the device is idle.
  • To restore scheduled fake calls after a device reboot where supported by the system.
Foreground service and notification permissions Examples: FOREGROUND_SERVICE, FOREGROUND_SERVICE_PHONE_CALL, POST_NOTIFICATIONS.
  • To run foreground services necessary to reliably trigger fake calls in the background.
  • To show notifications when a call is scheduled or when a foreground service is active, as required by Android.

2.3 Automatically Collected Information

When you use Shadow Call, some technical information may be collected automatically:

  • On-device technical data: The App may internally track logs (e.g., events related to schedules, errors, or call simulation state) to ensure correct behavior. These logs are stored locally and are primarily for debugging and reliability.
  • Third-party analytics and advertising data: Through SDKs such as Google Mobile Ads (AdMob), limited usage and device information may be collected automatically, such as:
    • Device identifiers (e.g., advertising ID)
    • Approximate location (e.g., based on IP)
    • App interactions (e.g., ad impressions, clicks)
    This data is processed by those third parties according to their own privacy policies (see Section 4).

We do not maintain our own backend server for tracking your usage across devices.

5. Data Storage, Retention, and Security

  • Local storage on your device: Caller profiles, schedules, app settings, and most logs are stored locally on your device. If you uninstall the App, this locally stored data is generally removed by the operating system. Some call log entries written to the system call log (if you enabled that feature) may remain as part of your device's call history.
  • Retention: We retain information only as long as necessary to provide the App's features, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, or enforce our agreements.
  • Security: We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect data processed by the App. However, no mobile application or online service can guarantee absolute security.

6. Your Choices and Controls

  • Permissions: You can grant or revoke individual permissions (e.g., Contacts, Call Log, Phone) at any time through your Android system settings. Revoking certain permissions may reduce the realism or functionality of fake calls (for example, disabling Contacts access will prevent contact suggestions).
  • Managing local data: You can delete caller presets and schedules from within the App's UI. You can uninstall the App at any time to remove its local data. System call log entries previously written (if allowed) may need to be removed through your device's call log interface.
  • Ads and personalized advertising: Depending on your region and device, you may see options in the App or in your system settings to control ad personalization (for example, "Opt out of Ads Personalization" or similar). For more detailed controls, consult your device settings and Google's privacy controls.
